Who we are

Clean air is a shared responsibility

The Air Purification Association is an unincorporated nonprofit association built around a simple belief: everyone deserves to breathe clean indoor air, regardless of the room they walk into.

As an unincorporated nonprofit association, we dedicate our efforts entirely to cleaner indoor environments — collecting, refurbishing, and redistributing professional-grade air filtration to the spaces and communities that need it most.

Our Mission

To measurably improve indoor air quality in homes, classrooms, workshops, and community spaces by putting effective filtration directly into the hands of the people who use those rooms every day.

Our Approach

We accept donated and surplus filtration equipment, ensure it works well, and match each unit to the environment it serves best — from HEPA home purifiers to DIY box-fan-and-MERV-13 kits.

Our Community

Beyond equipment, we channel funds to smaller, hyper-local nonprofits — multiplying our impact through organizations already trusted in their neighborhoods.

Our journey so far

Milestones that took us from a single donated purifier to billions of cubic feet of cleaner air.

The Beginning

An idea takes shape

Two founders, frustrated by poor indoor air in local classrooms and workshops, begin collecting and refurbishing surplus air purifiers to give away.

First Deployments

Units reach real rooms

The first HEPA purifiers and DIY box-fan kits are placed in homes and community rooms, proving that simple, well-matched equipment makes an immediate difference.

Scaling Up

Workshops & classrooms onboard

High-CFM portable systems and tabletop dust collectors expand our reach into woodshops and schools, dramatically increasing the volume of air processed.

Giving Back

Funding local allies

We begin redistributing funds — ultimately $1,400 across 18 smaller, localized nonprofits — to amplify clean-air work already happening on the ground.

Today

15.83 billion cubic feet cleaned

Our deployed systems have now processed 15.83 billion cubic feet of indoor air — and we are just getting started.
