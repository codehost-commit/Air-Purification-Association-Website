Who we are

Clean air is a shared responsibility

The Air Purification Association is an unincorporated nonprofit association built on one belief: everyone deserves clean indoor air, in every room they walk into.

As an unincorporated nonprofit association, we dedicate our efforts entirely to cleaner indoor environments, collecting, refurbishing and redistributing professional grade air filtration to the spaces that need it most.

Our Mission

To measurably improve indoor air quality in homes, classrooms, workshops and community spaces by putting effective filtration directly into the hands of the people who use those rooms every day.

Our Approach

We accept donated and surplus equipment, make sure it works well, and match each unit to the environment it serves best, from HEPA home purifiers to DIY box fan kits.

Our Community

Beyond equipment, we channel funds to smaller, hyper local nonprofits, multiplying our impact through organizations already trusted in their neighborhoods.

Our journey

From one purifier to billions of cubic feet

The milestones that took us from a single donated unit to clean air at scale.

The beginning

An idea takes shape

Two founders, frustrated by poor air in local classrooms and workshops, start collecting and refurbishing surplus air purifiers to give away.

First deployments

Units reach real rooms

The first HEPA purifiers and DIY box fan kits land in homes and community rooms, proving that simple, well matched equipment makes an immediate difference.

Scaling up

Workshops and classrooms onboard

High CFM portable systems and tabletop dust collectors expand our reach into woodshops and schools, sharply increasing the volume of air processed.

Giving back

Funding local allies

We begin redistributing funds, ultimately 1,400 dollars across 18 smaller local nonprofits, to amplify clean air work already happening on the ground.

Today

15.83 billion cubic feet cleaned

Our deployed systems have now processed 15.83 billion cubic feet of indoor air, and we are just getting started.
